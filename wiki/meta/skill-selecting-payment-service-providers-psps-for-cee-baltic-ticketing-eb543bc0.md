**Skill: Selecting Payment Service Providers (PSPs) for CEE/Baltic Ticketing**

**Topic:** Evaluating and selecting payment processors for event ticketing in Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Poland, Romania, Slovakia, and the Czech Republic.

**When to use:** When entering or optimizing payment operations for ticketing/events in CEE/Baltic markets, requiring support for cards and dominant local rails (BLIK, P24, bank-link, open banking).

**Procedure (5 steps):**
1.  **Define Priorities:** Rank must-have payment methods (e.g., BLIK in PL/CZ, P24 in CEE) against business model needs (SMB vs. enterprise ticketing volume).
2.  **Shortlist by Coverage:** Filter PSPs for explicit licensing and operational support across all 7 target markets; prioritize those with local entity licenses (e.g., EMI in LT).
3.  **Verify Current Status:** Contact shortlisted PSPs to confirm active licenses, operational capacity, and stability, especially after known restructurings (e.g., Kevin.'s 2024 changes).
4.  **Compare Fit:** Assess each PSP’s strength: enterprise acquiring strength vs. SMB transparency (pricing, API), and specialization (card processing vs. A2A open-banking).
5.  **Validate Enterprise Support:** For high-volume ticketing, directly confirm dedicated sales/account management and custom terms, as public info may only reflect SMB packages.

**Pitfalls:**
-   **Assuming Coverage:** A PSP serving "CEE" may not support all 7 specific markets or required local methods; always verify per-country.
-   **Overlooking Licensing Changes:** The payments sector is dynamic; a provider’s license or operational status can shift quickly (e.g., due to regulatory updates or M&A).
-   **Confusing SMB/Enterprise Fit:** Public pricing/support often targets SMBs; enterprise ticketing requires pre-sales engagement to secure needed acquiring terms and SLAs.
-   **Neglecting Direct Verification:** Relying solely on website data is insufficient; use contact forms/emails to validate current capabilities and speak to sales.
