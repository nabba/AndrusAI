# Estonian Forestry Statistical Data Sourcing

## Key Concepts

**Primary Data Authority**: The Estonian Environment Agency (Keskkonnaagentuur) is the central authority responsible for collecting, processing, and publishing forestry statistics in Estonia. Data is compiled and disseminated through the national statistical framework managed by Statistics Estonia (Statistikaamet).

**National Forest Inventory (NFI)**: The cornerstone of Estonian forestry data collection, established in 1999. The NFI is a sample-based survey that measures plots nationwide to estimate major forest characteristics including area, growing stock, increment, and species composition. It covers all land-use categories and ownership groups, including protected areas. Data from the last five years are used to prevent errors, and results are approximately 1.5 years old at publication.

**Administrative vs. Forest Accounts**: Estonia maintains two complementary statistical frameworks:
- **Administrative Forestry Statistics**: Long-term timeseries on forest area, growing stock, annual increment, protected forest share, and felling volumes
- **Forest Accounts**: A macroeconomic framework linking physical indicators (area, stock, increment, harvest) with monetary values and economic indicators (output, costs, value added)

**Data Sources Integration**: Forestry statistics are compiled from multiple streams:
- National Forest Inventory sample plots
- State Forest Management Centre (RMK) data on state forests
- Forest register data for registered immovables
- Foreign trade data (Statistics Estonia)
- Remote sensing (satellite and aerial measurements)
- Land cadastre information for private forest registration

**The Estonian Environmental Portal**: The primary public dissemination platform where all forestry data is published under "Forest and Current Status of Forest Information" section. This includes real-time forest information, interactive diagrams, downloadable datasets, and annual statistical summaries.

## Best Practices

**Methodological Rigor**: The NFI uses statistically robust sample selection to ensure representativeness and enable error estimation. Continuous plots are being developed to provide more current data between full inventory cycles, addressing the historical issue where some forest subcompartment data was more than 20 years old.

**Multi-Source Verification**: Data accuracy is ensured through cross-validation across sources—NFI ground truthing, remote sensing imagery, and administrative records from RMK and the forest register are integrated before publication.

**Public Access & Transparency**: All forestry datasets are published through the open data portal with API access, enabling third-party integration. Statistics Estonia provides standardized table codes (KK509, KK51, KK513, KK514, MM03, MM04, MM05, MM10) for consistent data retrieval.

**Temporal Consistency**: Long-term monitoring is maintained through continuous methodologies; current NFI data can be compared back to 1999, while some administrative statistics extend further back, supporting trend analysis for policy and carbon accounting purposes.

**Stakeholder Coverage**: The NFI explicitly includes all ownership categories—state-managed forests (RMK), private forests, and protected areas—ensuring comprehensive representation and avoiding bias toward any single forest management regime.

**Use for Multiple Purposes**: Data serves several concurrent functions: greenhouse gas inventory (LULUCF sector), forest accounting for Eurostat, real-time monitoring through the portal, and legal forest register maintenance for resource management.
