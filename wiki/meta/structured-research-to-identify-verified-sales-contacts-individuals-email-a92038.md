# **** Structured research to identify verified sales contacts (individuals, emails, LinkedIn) for reg

*kb: experiential | id: skill_experiential_a462dfbba9203881 | status: active | usage: 0 | created: 2026-04-23T22:59:33+00:00*

**Topic:** Structured research to identify verified sales contacts (individuals, emails, LinkedIn) for regional payment service providers (PSPs).

**When to Use:** For B2B sales, partnership, or market intelligence outreach targeting niche, geographically-defined financial technology companies where direct contact details are not centrally listed.

**Procedure:**
1.  **Define Scope:** Precisely specify the target region (e.g., CEE) and industry segment (e.g., regional PSPs for PLG).
2.  **Source Verification:** Use authoritative, tool-verifiable sources (e.g., official company websites, verified LinkedIn, reputable business databases like Tracxn). Avoid unverified directories or pattern-guessing.
3.  **Extract Key Fields:** For each identified company, extract: Legal Name, Website, Primary Sales Contact Email (or noted contact form), Head of Sales Name & LinkedIn URL, and a brief comment on product fit/status.
4.  **Triangulate & Validate:** Cross-check extracted details across at least two independent sources. Flag any company marked as "deadpooled" or inactive (e.g., via Tracxn).
5.  **Tabulate Output:** Compile results into a clean, filterable table. Explicitly mark cells where direct contacts are not publicly available. Do not invent or guess email patterns.

**Pitfalls:**
-   **Fabrication:** Never generate or infer email addresses from name patterns if not found publically. This destroys credibility.
-   **Stale Data:** Companies change leadership or cease operations. Always check recency and acquisition status via sources like Tracxn or news alerts.
-   **Scoping Error:** Ensure companies genuinely fit the "regional PSP" criteria; avoid including global players with a minor regional office.
-   **Source Quality:** Relying on single, outdated sources leads to high error rates. Prioritize primary sources (company sites) over aggregated lists.
-   **Over-Promising:** A cell marked "—" for contact indicates a gap, not a failure. It's preferable to an incorrect detail. Plan alternate outreach paths (e.g., general contact, LinkedIn).
