**Topic**  
Structured research to identify verified sales contacts (individuals, emails, LinkedIn) for regional payment service providers (PSPs), particularly in CEE/Baltics.

**When to Use**  
When requiring accurate, citation-backed sales outreach targets for B2B partnerships in specific geographic markets, especially where direct public emails are scarce and domain pattern inference is necessary.

**Procedure**  
1. **Define Scope & Index:** Clarify target region/industry (e.g., "CEE ticketing PSPs"). Search a master index/wiki for pre-researched lists to avoid duplication.  
2. **Triangulate Sources:** Use live web search (e.g., Brave) to locate vendor sites, then drill into "Contact," "Team," or "About" pages. Supplement with professional networks (LinkedIn public profiles) to identify sales leadership.  
3. **Extract & Infer:** Record confirmed details (name, title, LinkedIn URL). Where only a company domain is published, infer individual email patterns (e.g., `first@domain.com`) based on known employee examples from tools like RocketReach or Hunter.io—label these as *inferred*.  
4. **Cross-Verify:** Validate inferred patterns by checking multiple public profiles or company email directories. If no confirmation, mark as unverified best-guess.  
5. **Document with Citations:** Compile a structured table (PSP, URL, Sales Email, Head of Sales, HoS Email, LinkedIn, Comments). Cite every data point to its source (vendor site, LinkedIn, third-party tool) for transparency.

**Pitfalls**  
- **Over-reliance on Inferred Patterns:** Treat inferred emails as unconfirmed guesses; sending untested emails may violate anti-spam laws or harm sender reputation.  
- **Regional Nuance Ignored:** Local naming conventions, company structures, or privacy laws (e.g., GDPR) vary; generic global patterns often fail.  
- **Stale Data:** LinkedIn profiles and vendor sites become outdated quickly; always note the research date.  
- **Citation Gaps:** Failing to source every cell undermines credibility; if a detail cannot be cited, omit it or mark as "unfound."  
- **Compliance Risk:** Using scraped or inferred personal data may breach platform terms or data protection regulations—prioritize publicly shared info only.
