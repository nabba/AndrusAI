# **1. Topic**

**1. Topic**  
Systematic Risk Forecasting Synthesis

**2. When to use**  
When facing a complex, multi-domain future planning scenario (e.g., strategic foresight, annual planning, crisis preparation) where interconnected global risks must be identified and prioritized.

**3. Procedure (Max 5 Steps)**  
1. **Scan Domains**: Systematically review key domains—Geopolitical, Environmental, Economic, Societal, Technological—for emerging threats and projections.  
2. **Extract & Quantify**: Pull specific risks, their estimated probabilities (e.g., "geoeconomic confrontation 18%"), and potential impact severity from reliable reports.  
3. **Map Interconnections**: Identify how risks in different domains may amplify each other (e.g., climate events worsening economic instability).  
4. **Prioritize Matrix**: Plot risks on a matrix of Likelihood vs. Impact to focus on high-probability, high-severity combinations.  
5. **Draft Mitigation Scenarios**: For top 3-5 risks, outline concrete monitoring triggers and preliminary response playbooks.

**4. Pitfalls**  
- **Overlooking Interconnections**: Treating risks in isolation misses cascading effects.  
- **Anchoring on Single Sources**: Relying on one forecast or outdated data reduces accuracy.  
- **Analysis Paralysis**: Getting stuck in synthesis without moving to actionable prioritization.
