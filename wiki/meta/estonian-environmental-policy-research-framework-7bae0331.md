# Estonian Environmental Policy Research Framework

## Key Concepts

### Governance Structure
- **Ministry of Climate (established 2023)**: Central coordinating body for climate, energy, transport, mineral resources, environmental awareness, fisheries, and hunting policies. Integrates climate, energy, transport, building, and circular economy sectors under one umbrella.
- **Estonian Environment Agency**: Responsible for environmental monitoring, data collection, and publishing Estonian Environmental Reviews.
- **SEI Tallinn (Stockholm Environment Institute)**: Applied research institution conducting stakeholder engagement and capacity building in the Baltic Sea Region and EU.
- **Estonian Environmental Research Centre**: Research institution focused on environmental analysis and policy recommendations.

### Strategic Framework Documents
- **Estonian Environmental Strategy 2030**: Long-term environmental policy framework with health and quality of life goals.
- **Estonia 2035**: National strategy addressing climate-neutral energy production, energy security, sustainable transportation, and environmental awareness.
- **National Energy and Climate Plan (NECP) 2030**: Integrates 71 measures across energy and climate policy with binding targets.
- **Estonian Green Transition Action Plan 2023–2025**: Focuses on reducing negative environmental impacts, preserving ecosystems, and protecting biodiversity.
- **Fundamentals of Estonian Climate Policy until 2050 (FECP 2050)**: Long-term climate policy foundation.

### Key Targets
- **80% GHG reduction by 2050** (70% by 2030 compared to 1990 levels)
- **100% renewable electricity by 2030** to cover annual electricity demand
- **50% renewable share in total final energy consumption by 2030**
- **80% of heat energy from renewable sources**
- **Climate neutrality by 2050** (aligned with EU-wide target and Paris Agreement)
- **13% reduction in effort-sharing sector emissions by 2030** (compared to 2005)

## Best Practices

### Integrated Policy Planning
- Unify climate, energy, transport, building, and circular economy sectors under a single ministry for coherent policy development.
- Align national strategies with EU directives and international agreements while maintaining country-specific goals.
- Coordinate preparation, implementation, and supervision of climate plans through dedicated institutional arrangements.

### Evidence-Based Decision Making
- Use environmental indicators and metrics for both national and international policy guidance.
- Publish regular Environmental Overviews (every 4 years) with socio-economic background and environmental data.
- Develop dynamic energy performance certificates based on metered energy demand using 100% smart meter coverage.

### Legal Framework Development
- Enact comprehensive Climate Laws establishing legally binding targets for carbon neutrality and intermediate emissions reductions.
- Allocate clear responsibilities for implementation and tracking across sectors.
- Set explicit phase-out timelines for oil shale and other fossil fuels (phase out by 2035, end by 2040).

### Multi-Stakeholder Engagement
- Hold annual strategy days with key partners and stakeholders to review goal achievements.
- Integrate state budget strategy processes with environmental planning.
- Coordinate across ministries (Economy and Communications, Environment, Rural Affairs) for comprehensive plan development.

### Market-Based Incentives
- Implement renewable energy auctions to award market-based incentives.
- Introduce vehicle taxation linked to GHG emission levels and energy efficiency standards.
- Develop carbon pricing mechanisms aligned with energy and climate goals.
- Use taxation policy as a climate policy tool (e.g., pollution-class-dependent road usage fees for heavy vehicles).

### Research and Innovation Integration
- Implement energy sector research and development programmes to apply innovation achievements.
- Partner with universities (University of Tartu, Estonian University of Life Sciences) for knowledge transfer.
- Leverage digitalization leadership for building energy data improvements (e-construction platform).
