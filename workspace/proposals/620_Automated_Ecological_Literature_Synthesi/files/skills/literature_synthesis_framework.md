# Ecological Literature Synthesis Framework

## Purpose
To move beyond simple summarization toward critical synthesis of disparate ecological studies.

## Synthesis Steps
1. **Extraction**: Identify Core Variables (e.g., temperature, species count), Methodology, and Key Findings from fetched text.
2. **Comparison Matrix**:
   - **Consensus**: Findings that appear across multiple independent studies.
   - **Conflict**: Findings where methodologies or results diverge (e.g., Study A says warming increases biodiversity, Study B says it decreases it).
   - **Gaps**: Areas where current literature provides insufficient data.
3. **Quality Assessment**: Evaluate the 'reproducibility' and 'sample size' mentioned in the research (linking to `advanced_ecological_media_analysis__key_concepts_include_reproducibility`).
4. **Output Generation**: Structure reports by *theme* rather than by *paper* to facilitate rapid decision-making in crisis communication.
