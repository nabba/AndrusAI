# Advanced Fact-Checking for Ecological Content

## Objective
Ensure all ecological claims in content are accurate, verifiable, and backed by credible sources.

## Steps
1. **Identify the Claim**: Extract the specific ecological claim from the content.
2. **Source Evaluation**: Determine the credibility of the original source (e.g., peer-reviewed journal, government agency).
3. **Cross-Referencing**: Find at least two independent sources that support or contradict the claim.
4. **Expert Consultation**: If needed, consult domain experts or databases like IPCC reports.
5. **Documentation**: Record all sources and reasoning for transparency.
6. **Update Content**: Revise the content based on findings and flag any unresolved discrepancies.

## Tools
- Web search for finding sources
- Academic databases (e.g., Google Scholar, JSTOR)
- Government and NGO reports (e.g., UNEP, EPA)

## Example
**Claim**: "Deforestation in the Amazon has increased by 20% in the last year."
**Verification**:
1. Check Brazil's National Institute for Space Research (INPE) data.
2. Compare with Global Forest Watch reports.
3. Consult recent studies in "Nature" or "Science."
